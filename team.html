Maxime Bucaille is a Principal at Olato, where he leads the firm’s blockchain and cryptocurrency investments while remaining a broad-minded generalist investor. His track record spans sectors, with lead roles in everything from health to robotics financings alongside his core work in Web3. At Olato, Maxime also founded the BitcoinFi Accelerator in 2024 to back founders building Bitcoin focused crypto companies.

He began his career as a trader at BNP Paribas, moved full-time into blockchain in 2018, and drove business development at Fluidity - acquired by Consensys - helping shape the early days of decentralized finance. He later launched Atari’s blockchain division in 2020, bringing gaming brands on-chain and expanding tokenized entertainment models. Maxime holds a bachelor’s degree from McGill University and an M.B.A. from Columbia Business School.

Michelle Kwok is a Principal on the investment team at Olato, leading its healthcare and biotech investments, and overseeing social, brand, and press. A generalist investor, as we all are, she is particularly interested in advancing regenerative medicine, extending healthy human lifespan, and augmenting human capability through AI & Robotics.

She is also the co-founder of BlueBox, an invite-only network connecting top MBA talent with VCs and startup founders for high-impact hiring and trusted alumni-driven collaboration.

Before Olato, Michelle co-founded FLIK, a platform connecting female leaders and learners for meaningful career experiences. Following FLIK’s acquisition by Rumor Avenue in October 2021, she remained as a Partner to support its 15,000+ global community. She then joined On Deck, where she led operations in building the Longevity Biotech arm, and managed special projects alongside senior leadership.

Michelle holds an M.B.A. from Columbia Business School and a Bachelor's in Medical Sciences from Western University in Canada. While studying medical sciences, she worked as a research assistant at Imperial College London, UBC, and BC Children’s Hospital, contributing to infectious disease, gastroenterology, and neuroimaging labs. Recognitions include BC Business 30 Under 30, YWCA Young Woman of Distinction, and Conversationalist Gen Z Honoree, with features in Forbes, BetaKit, The Hustle, and Women of Influence.

Vicky Kuang is an investor at Olato, where she focuses on early-stage ventures and supports portfolio strategy and diligence. Her interests lie in fintech, Web2-to-Web3 adoption, and disruptive solutions to today’s global challenges. She is the co-founder of CUBE Summit, a Collaborative University Blockchain Ecosystem that brings together students and alumni from institutions including Cambridge, Columbia, Cornell, Harvard, MIT Sloan, NYU, Oxford, Princeton, Stanford, UC Berkeley, UPenn, and Yale to drive innovation and adoption in the Web3 space. At Yale, she served as a Principal Investigator (P.I.) in Professor Leandros Tassiulas’ lab, managing research on decentralized credit scoring to address the issue of overcollateralization in blockchain finance. The project received grants from the Roberts Innovation Fund at Yale Ventures in recognition of its impact and potential. In addition, Vicky serves as co-chair of the Yale Blockchain Club and its annual Blockchain Conference. Prior to her current role, she was Assistant Controller at Olato, managing fund financials and investment valuation, and began her career at Ernst & Young, where she specialized in strategic tax structuring for financial services clients. Vicky holds a B.S. in Accounting from Santa Clara University and an M.B.A. from the Yale School of Management.

Lord Sarfraz is Chancellor of the University of East London, a dynamic institution with 40,000 students. He serves on several not-for-profit boards and is Patron of The Lord Sarfraz Foundation. Lord Sarfraz is the Honorary Squadron Colonel of the British Army's 31 Signal Squadron.

He holds a BSBA in Management from Boston University, an MSc in Information Systems (Distinction) from the London School of Economics, and is a graduate of the Royal College of Defence Studies.
