BIOGRAPHY

[ Visionary / Investor / Educator ]

Tim Draper is not just an investor; he is a catalyst for global change, championing decentralization and the entrepreneurial spirit.

Born to be Wild

June 11, 1958: Timothy Cook Draper arrives and a year later, his father and mother move the family to California to join his grandfather at the first Silicon Valley venture capital firm, Draper, Gaither and Anderson, started in 1957.

His grandfather had previously led the economic reconstruction of Germany and Japan with the Marshall Plan after WWII, had served as the President of the Long Island Railroad, the President of the Mexican electric company, Undersecretary of the Army under Eisenhower and Undersecretary of State under Truman, and the first US Ambassador to NATO.

His father, Bill Draper became one of the pioneers of venture capital, building Sutter Hill Ventures, and then served under Reagan as Chairman of the Export-Import Bank of the US, and under Bush 41, served as the Administrator of UNDP. He then went on to create the first venture capital firm in India, Draper Richards, and the first venture philanthropy fund, DRK Foundation.

Tim Draper became the first third-generation venture-capitalist. Three of his children are also venture capitalists, adding to the Draper Dynasty. Through his career as a VC, Tim Draper has built a rare visionary and moonshot mindset that still fuels every bold prediction you'll find in the Media & Press section below.

Investing in the Future and Making history

Tim Draper graduated from Stanford in electrical engineering (1980), and received a Harvard MBA (1984), then after a brief stint working with Alex. Brown & Sons, founded Draper Associates in 1985, which he grew into what became DFJ in 1995.

What followed were the investments that changed everything — Hotmail (where Tim invented viral marketing), Baidu, Skype, Tesla, SpaceX, Twitch, and Bitcoin, which he purchased from the US Marshall's office in a public auction. These are the stories of intuition, risk, and legendary returns told in detail in the Legendary Investments section.

Building the Hero Factory

In 2011 Tim bought a historic 1927 hotel in downtown San Mateo and, in 2012, turned it into Draper University — the world's only live-in bootcamp that trains "Startup Heroes."

Over 6,000 alumni from 104 countries have graduated, launching 2,000+ companies. As of this writing, DU founders have built 8 unicorns already. Everything that happens inside those walls (and the global ecosystem it powers) is explored in The Network section.

The Journey of a Lifetime

From his 1958 birth into VC innovators who became VC royalty, through Stanford and Harvard, founding Draper Associates, the Bitcoin auction win, Draper University, his book, "The Startup Hero", the TV show, "Meet The Drapers", and Draper Associates' 40th anniversary in 2025 — every key milestone is mapped out with photos and exact dates in the interactive Life of a Hero timeline.

The Playbook He Lives By

These four unbreakable principles — Viral Marketing, Decentralization, The Startup Hero, and Free Markets — guide every investment and every speech. Read them in Tim’s own words in the Philosophy section.

The Show That Turned Pitching Into Prime Time

In 2017 Tim brought his entire family on screen and launched Meet The Drapers — now in Season 9, watched in 167 countries, investing $1 million prizes to the next wave of heroes. Watch the latest episodes, winner stories, and behind-the-scenes clips right now in The Show section.

Hotmail

The Invention of Viral Marketing

Sabeer Bhatia and Jack Smith pitched a web-based email. Tim suggested adding a footer message.

“I came up with that viral marketing idea, in Hotmail’s case.”

— Tim Draper

THESIS

"Can we use the product to sell itself?"

KEY NUMBERS

100,000 → 1M users in 5 months → 12M in 18 months

Marketing spend: $500,000

Exit: Microsoft $400M (1997)

THE Lesson

Let customers be your marketing team. The best products spread themselves.

Baidu

First Silicon Valley VC in China

Robin Li. One year old company. No one else would touch it.

“I invested in Robin Li because he is steady, consistent and gives great confidence that he can execute well.”

— Tim Draper

THESIS

“China will need its own search engine. Robin is the best engineer for the job.”

KEY NUMBERS

$9M for 28% stake‍

Today: 76% of China’s search market

THE Lesson

Global vision requires betting on local heroes early.

Skype

Killing the Telcos

Niklas Zennström and Janus Friis wanted to use P2P tech for voice. Telcos were charging dollars per minute.

“If voice becomes software, marginal cost goes to zero.”

— Tim Draper

THESIS

Internet will commoditize voice communication. Zero marginal cost = massive scale.

KEY NUMBERS

10% stake

‍eBay exit: $4.1B (2005)

Microsoft exit: $8.5B (2011)

THE Lesson

Disrupt monopolies by driving their marginal cost to zero.

Bitcoin

The Silk Road Auction

Bought 29,657 seized BTC when the entire VC industry ran away.

“Bitcoin frees people from trying to operate in a modern market economy with weak currencies.”

— Tim Draper (2014)

THESIS

New currency not tied to political force = pure freedom.

When regulators scare everyone else away — that’s your signal.

Philosophy

Tim's Principles

Viral Marketing

"Let the product sell itself"

Tim coined 'viral marketing' with Hotmail's email footer strategy. It changed how software grows forever: organic, exponential, and user-driven.

Decentralization

"Centralization is the past. Freedom is the future."

Advocating for a world where trust is distributed, not mandated. Governments should compete for citizens, not control them.

The Startup Hero

"Entrepreneurs are the heroes of the modern world."

They fight the status quo, risk everything, and build the future. Government and policy should support, not hinder them.

Free Markets

"Let the product sell itself"

Open markets and competition create the best outcomes for humanity. Regulation often stifles the very innovation we need.
